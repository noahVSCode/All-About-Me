Culture

Ethiopia

I am from Ethiopia. My dad and mom are from Addis Ababa. Because my mom and dad are from Ethiopia, I share some of that Ethiopian culture. I speak the Ethiopian national language, which is Amharic. If you want to say hello in Amharic, you say selam. I also celebrate Ethiopian Easter, and Ethiopian Christmas, becuase I am from Ethiopia. Ethiopia also has a different calendar! There are 13 months instead of 12, and the year is 8 years less than the American year! For example, if it is 2025 in America it is 2017 in Ethiopia!

Food

Ethiopian food is amazing. I could ramble on and on about this, but I'll keep it short. My 2 favorite Ethiopian foods, (I can't choose between them) are called tibs (ጥብስ, in Amharic) and kitfo (ክትፎ, in Amharic). If you forced me to choose, I would choose kitfo (barely), but they are both on my top 5 foods list for sure. Tibs is basically cooked beef with peppers served alongside it. It also comes in lamb and chicken. You sometimes eat tibs with ethiopian spices called awazie (pronounced: a-WA-ZEY) and mitmita (pronounced: MEET-MEET-a). Kitfo is also beef, but is served with butter, salt, and mitmita, which gives it a red coloring instead of tibs's brownish coloring. It is usually served raw instead of cooked. Sometimes, me being a child, I eat it cooked, but (don't say I'm crazy) I like it better when it is raw. Either way, it still tastes amazing. (And very spicy, like a ton of Ethiopian foods.) Kitfo is usually served with injera (a type of bread which I will get to later), ibie, which is cottage cheese (pronounced: I-ib), and gomen (pronounced: GO-MEHN), which is kale. Injera, a spongy bread, is the most popular food in Ethiopia, by far. It is served with alicha siga wot (beef stew), gomen, ibie, qeysir (beets, ቀይስር - in Amharic), dinich (potatoes, ድንች, in Amharic) Other ethiopian foods include beso (a drink made of honey, barley flour, etc. Also it is a breakfast made out of barley rolled up into balls), doro wot, (chicken stew, often eaten with injera) among others. Personally, I also like macaroni, jelly sandwiches, salmon, avacado salad, beans, but not much else. I'm a very picky eater (though you won't know it from what I've written, it's only because my mom is the best cook in the world by far.)
